note:

Most of us have been tyrannized for years, if not decades, by our institutions peddling "learning outcomes," "rubrics," and "assessments." On average we cynically go through the motions.

It's unfortunate that these ideas and practices are somewhat discredited because they came to us from bandwagoneers eager to please the Bush White House, because we SHOULD be more reflective and systematic about what and how we teach and what and how well students learn.

Those of us who are methodologically inclined have been the most offended by the status quo around outcomes because of years of being told "don't ask too many questions, just do it." We come from traditions where classifications and measurement procedures are carefully thought about and rigorously debated. That wasn't the case with learning outcomes.

So why am I advocating for them? Because done right, they can be pretty darn useful.

Process

Inductive LO Generation

The raw material was six course descriptions in MPP AI Courses.docx. Claude read these files along with background material on GAIT, etc. Initial prompt: here is what a learning outcome (LO) is, let's generate six for each course.

We iterated on content, where six was too stringent a constraint, which LOs might be too broad, etc. and settled on a list of 45 learning outcomes — verb-first, measurable, #Hashtag handles. For example:

  • #ClearThePath: (Student will be able to) [i]dentify norms/regulations that block beneficial AI deployment and propose reforms.
  • #TranslateTech: (Student will be able to) [c]ommunicate technical claims about AI systems, including their uncertainty, to non-technical decision-makers in briefing, testimony, and memo form.

The gap analysis

  • Asked: what do the courses fail to say for graduates governing the A(G)I transition?
  • Five structural gaps: international governance · politics of adoption · deciding under deep uncertainty · the existing legal landscape · algorithmic government today
  • 22 proposed outcomes, three candidate new courses (A International Order, B Politics of the Transition, C Governance Lab)
  • Every proposal cataloged as an LO note with provenance and seams

Taxonomy beginnings & what's next

  • Knols: atomic knowledge/skill units beneath each outcome; composition inside an LO, requires edges between LOs
  • Supporting layers: acronym glossary, wikilinked corpus, index note
  • Next: md→HTML sync script (make the notes authoritative), a synoptic editing view, tiers for a layered prerequisite graph
