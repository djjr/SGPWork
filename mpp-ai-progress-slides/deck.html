MPP-AI Curriculum

Where we are

Designing the MPP-AI concentration for the School of Government and Policy, under the GAIT initiative — Governing the AI Transition.

Six courses → outcomes → network → gaps → taxonomy.

From courses to outcomes

  • Faculty drafted six courses: Core (Building the AI Frontier) + C1–C5, from impacts through ethics, governance, evaluation, and adaptation

  • We inductively generated 45 learning outcomes — verb-first, measurable, one #Hashtag handle each

  • Cut across courses by 8 thematic clusters (Democracy & Legitimacy … Design & Synthesis)

  • Each LO now lives as its own note in LearningOutcomes/ — the intended source of authority

The network view

  • Nodes colored by course; grey links = same course, colored = shared cluster
  • New: dashed nodes = proposed outcomes, with dashed seam links showing where each proposal attaches
  • Toggle the proposal layer on/off; click to focus the gaps

The gap analysis

  • Asked: what do the courses fail to say for graduates governing the A(G)I transition?
  • Five structural gaps: international governance · politics of adoption · deciding under deep uncertainty · the existing legal landscape · algorithmic government today
  • 22 proposed outcomes, three candidate new courses (A International Order, B Politics of the Transition, C Governance Lab)
  • Every proposal cataloged as an LO note with provenance and seams

Taxonomy beginnings & what's next

  • Knols: atomic knowledge/skill units beneath each outcome; composition inside an LO, requires edges between LOs
  • Supporting layers: acronym glossary, wikilinked corpus, index note
  • Next: md→HTML sync script (make the notes authoritative), a synoptic editing view, tiers for a layered prerequisite graph
